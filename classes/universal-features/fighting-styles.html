Fighting Styles

Credit to LaserLlama for many of the Fighting Styles

These are the Fighting Styles, where each indicates what classes can take them.If a class is marked with an asterisk, it means it is a special subclass that grants them the ability to choose a fighting style. Which subclass grants the fighting style is specified at the Fighting Style, and on the subclass' own page.

Arcane Warrior

Effect: You learn two cantrips of your choice from the wizard spell list. They count as Magus spells for you, and Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for them. Whenever you gain a level in this class, you can replace one of these cantrips with another cantrip from the wizard spell list.

Available to: Magus

Featherweight Fighting

Effect: You are an expert at staying on your toes, and moving quickly about the battlefield. As long as you are not wearing medium or heavy armor or using a shield, and only wielding light weapons, you gain a +2 bonus to your damage rolls with light weapons, and your movement speed increases by 5ft.

Available to: College of Battle Bard *, Fighter, Hemomancer, Ranger, Vigilante Rogue *

Mariner

Effect: As long as you not wearing heavy armor or using a shield, you have a swimming speed and a climbing speed equal to your normal speed, and you gain a +1 bonus to armor class.

Available to: Fighter, Ranger

Shielded Warrior

Effect: Shields that are in your hands are also a weapon that deal 1d4 bludgeoning (if it is a light shield) and 1d6 bludgeoning (if it is a medium or heavy shield). They also have the nonlethal trait.

Available to: Path of the Berserker Barbarian *, Fighter, Paladin

Archery

Effect: You can choose to gain a +2 to all your attack rolls with a ranged weapon attack. Doing so reduces your speed by half until the start of your next turn. You cannot use this if you have moved more than half of your speed this turn.

Available to: Fighter, Hemomancer, Magus, Oath of Silence Paladin * , Ranger, Vigilante Rogue *

Finesse Fighting

Effect: You have trained in the classical art of light swordplay. When wielding a finesse weapon in one hand, and nothing in your other hand, you gain a +1 bonus to both your attack rolls and a +1 bonus to your Armor Class.

Available to: Fighter, Ranger

Marksmanship

Effect: The normal range of your ranged weapon attacks with weapons with either the Range or Thrown propperty is increased by a number of feet equal to 5 times your Proficiency Bonus, and the long-range is increased by a number of feet equal to 15 times your Proficiency Bonus.

Available to: Fighter, Ranger, Oath of Silence Paladin *

Slick Punches

Effect: Your unarmed attacks have the finesse property. Additionally, on your turn, if neither of your hands is wielding anything, you can choose to expend half of your movement speed, if available, to give your hands the Parry trait until the start of your next turn. It counts as wielding two weapons with the Parry property when using this ability.

Available to: Fighter, Ranger, Vigilante Rogue *

Blessed Warrior

Effect: You learn two cantrips of your choice from the cleric spell list. They count as paladin spells for you, and Charisma is your spellcasting ability for them. Whenever you gain a level in this class, you can replace one of these cantrips with another cantrip from the cleric spell list.

Available to: Paladin

Grappler

Effect: When you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack, you can use a bonus action on that turn to attempt to grapple them. You must have a free hand in order to initiate the grapple. In addition, you can drag a grappled creature up to your full movement speed if they are your size or smaller.

Available to: Fighter

Mounted Warrior

Effect: Once per turn, when you hit a creature with a melee attack while riding a mount, you can force the target to make a Strength saving throw. Save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength modifier. On a failure, it is knocked prone.

Available to: Fighter, Paladin

Tunnel Fighting

Effect: Once per turn, you can make an opportunity attack without using your reaction.

Available to: Fighter, Paladin Ranger

Blind Fighting

Effect: You have blindsight with a range of 10 feet. Within that range, you can effectively see anything that isn't behind total cover, even if you're blinded or in darkness. Moreover, you can see an invisible creature within that range, unless the creature successfully hides from you.

Available to: Fighter, Hemomancer, Paladin, Ranger, Assailant Rogue *

Great Weapon Fighting

Effect: While you are wielding a heavy weapon you get +2 to your damage rolls, and you have advantage on Athletics checks to shove

Available to: Path of the Berserker Barbarian *, Fighter, Hemomancer, Paladin,

Natural Weaponeer

Effect: If you have a natural weapon in each hand, like claws, they have the finesse and light property for the purposes of two-weapon fighting. Additionally, you get a +1 to damage roll with natural weapons.

Available to: Fighter, Ranger, Assailant Rogue *

Thrown Weapon Fighting

Effect: You can draw a weapon that has the thrown property as part of the attack you make with the weapon. In addition, when you hit with a ranged attack using a thrown weapon, you gain a +2 bonus to the damage roll.

Available to: Fighter, Ranger

Close Quarter Shooter

Effect: When making a ranged attack while you are within 5 feet of a hostile creature, you do not have disadvantage on the attack roll. Your ranged attacks ignore half cover and three-quarters cover against targets within 30 feet of you. You have a +1 bonus to damage rolls on ranged attacks.

Available to: College of Battle Bard *, Fighter, Hemomancer, Oath of Silence Paladin *, Ranger,

Heavy Attacks

Effect: When you roll a 1 or 2 on a damage die for an attack you make with a melee weapon that you are wielding with two hands, you can reroll the die and must use the new roll, even if the new roll is a 1 or a 2. You must be using both hands to wield it.

Available to: Path of the Berserker Barbarian *, Fighter, Hemomancer, Magus, Paladin

Piercing Shot

Effect: You get +2 to damage rolls with two-handed ranged weapons

Available to: Fighter, Ranger, Oath of Silence Paladin *

Two Weapon Fighting

Effect: When you engage in two-weapon fighting, you can add your ability modifier to the damage of the second attack.

Available to: College of Battle Bard *, Fighter, Hemomancer, Magus, Ranger, Assailant Rogue *, Vigilante Rogue *

Defense

Effect: While you are wearing armor, you gain a +1 bonus to AC.

Available to: Fighter, Magus, Paladin

Improvised Fighting

Effect: You are a maser of fighting dirty and underhanded. You gain proficiency with improvised weapons. Once per turn, when you make an attack using a non-magical improvised weapon, you can choose to add two more of the weapon's damage dice. When you do this the improvised weapon is destroyed and rendered useless for any further attacks.

Available to: Fighter

Power Attack

Effect: When you make a melee attack with a weapon you are proficient with, before you make the attack roll, you can choose not to add your proficiency bonus to the attack roll. If the attack hits, you add double your proficiency bonus to the attack's damage. You cannot do this if you are using the finesse property of the weapon.

Available to: Fighter, Paladin

Unarmed Fighting

Effect: Your unarmed strikes can deal bludgeoning damage equal to 1d6 + your Strength modifier. When you successfully start a grapple, you can deal 1d4 bludgeoning damage to the grappled creature. Until the grapple ends, you can also deal this damage to the creature whenever you hit it with a melee attack.

Available to: Path of the Berserker Barbarian *, Fighter, Ranger

Druidic Warrior

Effect: You learn two cantrips of your choice from the druid spell list. They count as ranger spells for you, and Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for them. Whenever you gain a level in this class, you can replace one of these cantrips with another cantrip from the druid spell list.

Available to: Ranger

Interception

Effect: When a creature you can see hits you or a target that is within 5 feet of you with an attack, you can use your reaction to reduce the damage the target takes by 1d10 + twice your proficiency bonus (to a minimum of 0 damage). You must be wielding a shield or a simple or martial weapon to use this reaction.

Available to: Fighter, Paladin, Ranger

Protection

Effect: When a creature you can see attacks a target other than you that is within 5 feet of you, you can use your reaction to impose disadvantage on the attack roll. You must be wielding a shield.

Available to: Fighter, Paladin

Versatile Fighting

Effect: When wielding a versatile weapon, you gain a +1 bonus to attack rolls when wielding it one-handed, and a +1 bonus to damage rolls when wielding it two-handed. In addition, as a bonus action, you can use your free hand to make an unarmed strike or shove action.

Available to: Fighter, Hemomancer, Magus, Paladin, Ranger

Dueling

Effect: When you are wielding a melee weapon in one hand and no other weapons, you gain a +2 bonus to damage rolls with that weapon.

Available to: College of Battle Bard *, Fighter, Hemomancer, Magus, Paladin, Ranger, Assailant Rogue *, Vigilante Rogue *

Lucky Strike

Effect:When you score a critical with your weapon attacks, you can add 1 more of your weapon's damage dice to the damage roll.

Available to: Path of the Berserker Barbarian *, Fighter

Quick Loader

Effect: You ignore the loading property and you get a +1 to your damage rolls with weapons that have the loading property.

Available to: Fighter, Oath of Silence Paladin *
