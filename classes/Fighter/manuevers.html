Maneuvers

Credit to LaserLlama for many of the Maneuvers

These are the Manuevers available to the Fighter. They are categorized into Battlefield Maneuvers, Defensive Maneuvers, General Maneuvers, Melee Maneuvers, Ranged Maneuvers, Skill Maneuvers and Unarmed Maneuvers.

Battlefield Maneuvers

These are the maneuvers you can use to perform combat utility and make strategic and tactical deeds.

Bait and Switch

When you’re within 5 feet of an ally on your turn, you can expend one superiority die and switch places with that ally, provided you spend at least 5 feet of movement. This movement doesn’t provoke opportunity attacks. Roll the superiority die. Until the start of your next turn, the ally gains a bonus to AC equal to the number rolled.

Blinding Shot

As a bonus action on your turn, you can expend a superiority die and throw sand or dirt at a creature within 5 feet and force them to make a Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, the creature takes damage equal to your superiority die roll and is blinded until the start of your next turn.

Commander's Strike

When you take the Attack action on your turn, you can forgo one of your attacks and use a bonus action to direct one of your companions to strike. When you do so, choose a friendly creature who can see or hear you and expend one superiority die. That creature can immediately use its reaction to make one weapon attack, adding the superiority die to the attack's damage roll.

Feinting Attack

You can expend one superiority die and use a bonus action on your turn to feint, choosing one creature within 5 feet of you as your target. You have advantage on your next attack roll against that creature before the end of your turn. If that attack hits, add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll.

Rally

On your turn, you can use a bonus action and expend one superiority die to bolster the resolve of one of your companions. When you do so, choose a friendly creature who can see or hear you. That creature gains temporary hit points equal to the superiority die roll + your Charisma modifier.

Defensive Maneuvers

These are the manuvers you use to bolster your own protection.

Evasive Footwork

When you move, you can expend one superiority die, rolling the die and adding the number rolled to your AC until you stop moving.

Iron Skin

When you are hit with a weapon attack, you can expend a superiority die to reduce the damage taken by an amount equal to two rolls of your superiority die.

Kip Up

When you are prone, you can expend 5ft of movement and expend a superiority die to get up from being prone without needing to spend any movement on it. You then roll your superiority die and add the roll to your AC until the start of your next turn.

Parry

When another creature damages you with a melee attack, you can use your reaction and expend one superiority die to reduce the damage by the number you roll on your superiority die + your Dexterity modifier.

Second Wind

As a bonus action on your turn, you can expend one superiority die immediately granting yourself temporary hit points equal to two rolls of your superiority die.

Melee Maneuvers

These are the maneuvers you can use with melee weapon attacks.

Lunging Attack

When you make a melee weapon attack on your turn, you can expend one superiority die to increase your reach for that attack by 5 feet. If you hit, you add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll.

Restraining Attack

Immediately after you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack on your turn, you can expend one superiority die and use a bonus action to grapple the target. Add the superiority die to your Athletics check. The target is also restrained while grappled in this way.

Riposte

When a creature misses you with a melee attack, you can use your reaction and expend one superiority die to make a melee weapon attack against the creature. If you hit, you add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll.

Sweeping Attack

When you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to damage another creature with the same attack. Choose another creature within 5 feet of the original target and within your reach. If the original attack roll would hit the second creature, it takes damage equal to the number you roll on your superiority die. The damage is of the same type dealt by the original attack.

Ranged Maneuvers

These are the maneuvers you can use with ranged weapon attacks.

Snipe

You can expend a superiority die when making a ranged attack to ignore the penalty of making ranged attacks at long range, and add the superiority die to the damage roll of the attack, if you hit. Additionally, when you take the attack action an make a ranged attack as the first attack of that attack action, you can choose to forego the rest of your attacks you make as part of that action to get a bonus to your attack roll equal to +2 per attack foregone.

Rapid Fire

As a bonus action, you can expend a superiority die and make a ranged weapon attack. You can draw a thrown weapon as part of making this attack. If you hit, add the superiority die to the attack’s damage roll.

Skill Maneuvers

These are the maneuvers you can use with skill checks, ability checks and saving throws.

Adapt

When you make an Athletics or Acrobatics check to resist the negative effects of a hostile environment, you can expend one superiority die and add the superiority die to the result of the skill check.

Ambush

When you make a Stealth check or an initiative roll, you can expend one superiority die and add the die to the roll.

Feat of Strength

When you make an Athletics check you can expend one superiority die and add the superiority die to the result of your roll.

Lightstep

Whenever you make a Sleight of Hand or a Stealth check, you can expend one superiority die and add it to the result of your roll.

Regal Bearing

When you make a History, Insight, or Persuasion check, you can expend one of your superiority dice and add it to the result of your roll.

Silver Tongued

When you make a Deception check or a Persuasion check, you can expend one superiority die, and add the superiority die to the ability check.

Skilled Rider

When you make an Animal Handling check, or your mount is forced to make a saving throw, you can expend one of your superiority dice and add the superiority die to the result of the ability check or saving throw.

Tactical Assement

When you make a History, an Investigation, or an Insight check, you can expend one superiority die and add the superiority die to the result of your ability check.

Unarmed Maneuvers

These are the maneuvers you can use with unarmed weapon attacks.

Drop Kick

When you hit a creature with an unarmed attack, you can force a Strength Save on them, and if they fail you push them away from you a number of feet equal to your Strength Modifier + your superiority die (round up to the closest 5ft) and they fall prone.

Suplex

When you hit a creature with an unarmed attack, that you are grappling, you can force a Strength Save on that creature, and if they fail you move the creature onto the opposite spot of where they were relative to you, they fall prone, you add your superiority dice to the damage roll, and you end your grapple.

Tackle

When you succeed in a grapple check against a creature that is no larger than your own size and that you have moved towards in a straight line for at least 10ft, you can force a Strength Save onto that creature. If they fail, you and the creature are moved, in the direction the target creature is facing in relation to you, by a number of feet equal Your Strength Modifier + your Superiority Die (rounding up to the closest 5ft). The creature then takes Bludgeoning Damage equal to your Superiority Die + Your Strength Modifier, and you both fall prone.
