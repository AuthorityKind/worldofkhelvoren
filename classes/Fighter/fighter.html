Hit Dice

Hit Dice: 1d10 per Fighter level

Hit Points at 1st Level: 10 + your Constitution modifier

Hit Points at higher levels: 6 + your Constitution modifier per Fighter level after 1st

Proficiencies

Armor: Light, Medium, and Heavy

Shields: Light, Medium, and Heavy

Weapons: All Simple and Martial Weapons, 1 exotic weapon of your choice

Tools: None

Saving Throws: Strength, Constitution

Skills: Choose two from the following list: Acrobatics, Animal Handling, Athletics, History, Insight, Intimidation, Perception, Survival

Languages: None

Class Features

Fighting Style

You adopt a particular style of fighting as your specialty. Choose one Fighting Style available to the Fighter. You can't take a Fighting Style option more than once, even if you later get to choose again. When you reach 13th level, you can choose another fighting style.

Combat Superiority

Beginning at 1st level, your skill with the armaments of war sets you apart from other warriors, even other adventurers. This skill with arms is represented with special maneuvers that you can use in combination with your attacks, and are fueled with a pool of special superiority dice. See the end of this class description for a list of maneuvers.

Superiority Dice

You gain three superiority dice, which are d6's, that represent your skill in battle. To use a maneuver you must expend one of these dice, and you regain all expended superiority dice when you finish a short or long rest. As you gain fighter levels, you gain more superiority dice, and your superiority dice increase in size, as indicated in the Superiority Dice and Die Size columns of the fighter table.

Manuevers Known

You learn two maneuvers of your choice from the list of maneuvers at the end of this class description. The Maneuvers Known column of the Fighter table shows when you learn more maneuvers of your choice. Additionally, when you gain a level in this class, you can choose one of the maneuvers you know and replace it with another maneuver of your choice.

Saving Throws

If a maneuver requires a creature to make a saving throw, your maneuver saving throw DC is calculated as follows:

Maneuver Save DC= 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength or Dexterity modifier (your choice).

Action Surge

Starting at 2nd level, you can push yourself beyond your normal limits for a moment. On your turn, you can take one additional action on top of your regular action and a possible bonus action. Once you use this feature, you must finish a short or long rest before you can use it again. Starting at 17th level, you can use it twice before a rest, but only once on the same turn.

Know Your Enemy

Beginning at 2nd level, you can observe or interact with friends and foes alike, learning their strengths and weaknesses in comparison to your own. As an action, choose a creature that you can see within 60ft, and one of its attributes from the following list: Strength score, Dexterity score, Constitution score, Armor Class, current hit points, or proficiency bonus. You immediately learn if the creature is your equal, superior, or inferior in that regard to the attribute you chose. Once you target a creature with this ability, you cannot target that same creature again until you finish a long rest. Upon reaching 13th level in this class, you can use this feature as either an action or bonus action on your turn.

Martial Archetype

At 3rd level, you choose an archetype that you strive to emulate in your combat styles and techniques. You gain a feature from your archetype this level, and then again at 7th, 10th, 15th and 18th level.

● Arcane Archer

● Echo Knight

● Legionnaire

● Spellscorn

● Bannerlord

● Eldrith Knight

● Psi Warrior

● Temporal Drifter

● Cavalier

● Enforcer

● Rune Knight

● Champion

● Gunslinger

● Samurai

Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 4th level, and again at 6th, 8th, 12th, 14th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature. Alternatively, you can take a feat you meet the prerequisites for.

When you gain Ability Score Improvement from this class, you can do one of the following, as you shift the focus of your martial practice:

• Replace a fighting style you know with another fighting style available to fighters.

• You can replace one maneuver you know with a different maneuver.

Extra Attack

Beginning at 5th level, you can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn. The number of attacks increases to three when you reach 11th level in this class and to four when you reach 20th level in this class. If you already have the Extra Attack feature from another class, you can gain both one Fighting Style from the Fighter and learn an additional Maneuver. If you reach 11th or 20th level in this class with Extra Attack from another class, you still gain the extra third and fourth attack.

Indomitable

At 9th level, you can persist through most dangers. When you make a saving throw that you are proficient in, you expend a superiority die and add it to the saving throw. You can do this after you have made the saving throw, but before you know the outcome of the roll. Additionally, choose a Saving throw you are not Proficient with. You gain proficiency with that Saving Throw.

Battle Ready

Beginning at 13th level, the spark of a fight can light the fire of war within you, even when the flames have dwindled. When you roll initiative, and you have any number of expended Superiority Dice, you can choose to regain a number of expended Superiority Dice equal to half of your total number of Superiority Die but it cannot exceed your total number of Superiority Dice. You can use this once and need to take a Long rest to be able to do so again.

Unbreakable Mettle

At 17th level, you can suffer through nearly everything thrown at you. When you make a Saving Throw and you use your Indomitable feature on that Saving Throw, then, if the Saving Throw is used to determine if you take full or half damage, you then also take only half the damage on a failed save and no damage on a successful save. If the effect that you made a Saving Throw against had any other effect that occurred when you succeed the Saving Throw, you can choose to forego it.

Battle Master

Beginning at 20th level, you have become the apex combatant. If you roll a number lower than either your Strength or Dexterity modifier (you choose) on your Superiority Dice, you do not expend a usage of your Superiority Die and the dice is instead equal to your Strength or Dexterity modifier (you choose). Additionally, once per turn, when you use a Maneuver, you can choose to not have to expend a Superiority Die, and instead have either your Strength or Dexterity Modifier be the result of what would have been your rolled Superiority Die.
