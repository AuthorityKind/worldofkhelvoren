Fighter Subclass

Spellscorn

Original Subclass credit to Fungal Brews

Bonus Proficiencies

When you choose this archetype at 3rd level, you gain proficiency in one of the following skills of your choice: Arcana, History, Religion

Null

When you choose this archetype at 3rd level, you manifest a magical dead zone around yourself. While you are conscious, you have resistance to damage dealt from cantrips and spells cast with a 1st-level slot. At 7th level, you also have resistance to damage dealt by spells of 2nd and 3rd level, at 10th level, you also have resistance to damage dealt by spells of 4th and 5th level, at 15th level, you also have resistance to damage dealt by spells of 6th and 7th level, and at 18th level you have resistance to all magical damage.

Additionally, when you see a creature cast a spell within 60ft of you, you can use your reaction to lower the DC of the spell (if the spell involves a Saving Throw to be made) or the attack roll of the spell (if the spell involves an attack roll to be made by the caster) by a number equal to half of your Fighter Level. You can do this a number of times equal to half of your Constitution Modifier (rounding up), and you get your uses back after a long rest. Alternatively, you can expend 3 Supperiorty Dice to regain a use of this ability.

You also have a Null DC, which is used any time a Saving Throw is made against any of your Spellscorn Features. Your Null DC is calculated as such:

Null Save DC= 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier

Wary Vigiliance

Begining at 3rd level, you can get an easy read on the spellcasters, knowing what they have at their disposal. When you use your Know Your Enemy feature, you can also choose to learn any of the following attributes of the target creature, if they are able to cast spells, where instead of having it compared to you, you just learn the exact number of propperty of that you are learning: the level is the highest level spell they are able to cast, the ability score is used for their spellscasting, how many remaning spell slots of a given spell slot level do they have left, their Spell DC, their Spell Attack Modifier.

When you reach 7th level, and when you have used Know Your Enemy to discern what Ability Score is used for their spellcasting, you then gain advantage on Saving Throws against spell cast from that creature for 1 minute. You can only benefit from this ability once at a time.

Weavecutter

Begining at 7th level, you strike through magic effortlessly. Your weapon attacks are considered magical for the purpose of overcoming resistance. Additionally, when you make a weapon attack roll, you ignore any additions the targets AC granted by spells, such as Shield or Mage Armor.

Evoke the Void

At 7th level, you can momentarily summon the nothingness to sprawl out from you. As an Action, you can Evoke the Void, which lasts for 1 minute, or if you end it early with a Bonus Action. An Aura of Null is emit out from you, going out 15ft from you. When you first activate this ability, every creature, except yourself, in your Aura of Null has to make a Constitution Save against your Null Save DC. If they fail, they take your Fighter Level + your Constitution Modifier in Force Damage, and half as much if they suceed. Then, while this ability is active, if a creature moves into your Aura of Null or ends its turn within your Aura of Null, they take Force Damage equal to your Constitution Modifier.

Each time a creature, who is capable of casting spells, is damaged by this ability, they take additional Force Damage equal to a roll of your Supperiorty Die. If a creature, capable of casting spells, is also concentrating on a spell, they instead take additional Force Damage equal to two rolls of your Supperiorty Die.

You can use this feature once, and need to take a Long Rest to be able to use it again.

Mages' Bane

At 10th level, you wield great powers of nullification, making you capable of shutting down magic that only highly gifted magicians should be able to. When you score a critical with a weapon attack, and the target of your attack is under the effects of a spell, it also suffers the effects of having Dispel Magic cast on it, at a level equal to half of your Fighter Level (to a maximum of a 9th level spell), where Constitution is used as your Spellcasting Ability Score for the purposes of the spell.

Arcane Absorbtion

Also at 10th level, weaker magic is broken down into its most basic fundemental components and reformed into a barrier for you. When you are affected by a spell of 2nd level or lower, make a DC 10 Constitution Ability check. If you succeed, you are completely immune to the effects of that spell, and you gain temporary HP equal to your Supperiorty Die + Your Consitution Modifer times the spell level. These temporary HP last for 1 minute. When you are about to make the Constitution Ability Check, you can expend a usage of your Null feature to automatically fail the check.

When you reach 15th level, you instead absorb spells of 3rd level or lower, and when you reach 18th level, you instead absorb spell of 4th level or lower.

Relic Breaker

At 15th level, you can corrode the magic that flows through enchanted items and treasures. As an action, you can touch a magic item and make a Constitution Ability Check, and if you suceed, the magic item looses its magical propperties for a number of days equal how much your Ability Check exceeded the DC by (minimum of 1) and you gain Temporary HP which lasts 1 hour. The DC of the Ability Check and the number of Temporary HP gained depends on the items variety, and is specified in the table below. Alternatively, you can choose to spend 1 minute instead of an action to use this ability. Doing so lets you add your Superiority Die to the Ability Check made.

Eradication of Magic

At 18th level, your very touch can rob creatures of their magic, potentially having their magical powers leave them for a long time. Once per turn as an action, or in place of a weapon attack, you touch one creature who can cast spells and force them to make a Constitution Save against your Null Save DC. If they fail, they choose spell slots to lose until the combined level of spell slots lost equal or exceed your Fighter Level + your Constitution Modifer.

For example, if your Fighter Level is 18 and your Constitution Modifier is +2, then the target creature must choose a number of spell slots, which spell slot level add up to be equal to over exceed your Fighter Level + your Constitution Modifier, which in this example is 20. So the caster could for example choose to sacrifice four 1st-level spell slots, three 2nd-level spells slots, two 3rd-level spell slots and one 4th-level spell slot, which would have a combined total that is equal to this examples Fighter Level + Constitution Modifier, which is 20.

If the target creature is not able to meet or exceed your Fighter Level + your Constitution Modifer with the spell slots that they have left, they then loose all remaining spell slots and become unable to both cast any spell, including spells that do not requirea spell slot to be expended, and they become unable to regain spell slots for number of days equal to the difference between their remaining spell slots' level combined, that could not meet or exceed your Fighter Level + Your Constitution Modifier, and your Fighter Level + your Constitution Modifier.

You can use this feature once, and need to take a long rest to be able to use it again. Alternatively, you can expend four Supperiorty Dice to regain a use of this ability.
