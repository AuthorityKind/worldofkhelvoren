Fighter Subclass

Gunslinger

Original Subclass Credit to Matt Mercer

Firearm Proficiency

Starting when you choose this archetype at 3rd level, you gain proficiency with firearms, allowing you to add your proficiency bonus to attacks made with firearms.

Gunsmithing

Upon choosing this archetype at 3rd level, you gain proficiency with Tinker’s Tools. You may use them to craft ammunition at half the cost, repair damaged firearms, or even draft and create new ones (DM’sdiscretion). Some extremely experimental and intricate firearms are only available through crafting.

Adept Marksman

When you choose this archetype at 3rd level, you have an innate sense for using your firearms. You have a pool of Grit points, with a total number of Grit equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of 2). When you take a long rest, you regain all spent Grit. You can use Grit for the following abilities:

● When you make an attack with a Firearm, you can spend 2 Grit to make the attack with advantage.

● When you roll your Superiority Dice in association with a maneuver where you use a Firearm, you can spend 1 Grit to roll the superiority dice twice and take the higher roll. Alternatively, you can spend 2 Grit to then also add your Wisdom Modifier to the roll of your Superiority Dice.

● When you make an Insight, Perception, or Intimidation check, you can spend 1 Grit to add your Wisdom modifier to the roll.

Quick Draw

When you reach 7th level, you add your Wisdom modifier to your initiative. You can also stow a firearm, then draw another firearm as a single object interaction on your turn.

Resting Moxie

Also at 7th level, a quick rest lets you recover much of your spent energy. When you take a short rest, you regain a number of expended Grit points equal to half of your Wisdom Modifier (rounding up) and if you spend 1 or more Hit Dice to regain HP, you regain additional HP equal to your Fighter Level.

Rapid Repair

Upon reaching 10th level, you learn how to quickly attempt to fix a jammed gun. You can spend 1 Grit to attempt to repair a misfired (but not broken) firearm as a bonus action. Additionally, when your Firearm misfires, you can spend 3 Grit to negate the misfire property for that attack.

Lightning Reload

Starting at 15th level, you can reload any firearm as a bonus action. Additionally, when you reduce a creature to 0HP with a Firearm or score a critical hit with a Firearm, you regain 1 expended Grit.

Viceous Intent

At 18th level, you now critically hit on 19 and 20 with weapon attacks made with firearms. Additionally, whenever you score a critical hit on an attack with a firearm, the target additionally suffers half of the damage from the attack at the end of its next turn.
